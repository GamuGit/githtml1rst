 What is Digital Citizenship 

    
 Digital citizenship means using technology responsibly and respectfully.

        It involves protecting personal information and staying safe online.
        
        Good digital citizens communicate kindly and think before they post.
        
        They follow laws, respect others’ rights, and give credit for others’ work.
        
        Practicing digital citizenship helps create a positive and secure online community.


 Cyberbullying 


 Cyberbullying is a serious issue affecting many 

students today. It involves the use of technology

to harass or intimidate others, leading to significant

 emotional distress. Recognizing this problem is the

  first step toward fostering asupportive 

online environment for all users.

 Impact of Cyberbullying 


Cyberbullying can cause deep emotional pain, leading to stress, anxiety, and depression.

    Victims may lose confidence and withdraw from social or academic activities.
    
    It can damage reputations and relationships, both online and offline.
    
    In severe cases, cyberbullying may lead to self-harm or suicidal thoughts.
    
    The lasting effects can harm both the victim’s mental health and overall well-being.
